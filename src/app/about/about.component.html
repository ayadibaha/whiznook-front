Whiznook is on the mission to make building teams more fun and effective.

We, at Whiznook, believe that our hiring industry got it backwards. We live in the world that makes professional candidates write up a page with all their relevant skills, spam as many companies with it and cross fingers for a hit; then endure hours of interviews and hopefully land a job. That’s not, however, where the story ends. According to a 2018 study by Mercer, more than 30% of the hired employees will not last a year. Another study revealed that replacing high-talented employee will cost a company 200% of the employee’s annual salary.

So what’s the problem? Why we are so bad at assessing people for the right roles?

The problem is that interviews, and other personality and behavioral assessments, are based on intentional and often skillful branding of ourselves. After a few months, our guards come down and our true personalities come forward. This is true for both the hiring team and the potential candidate.

Our solution is to remove team players’ guards from the get-go. We created a game interview - an immersive, interactive approach to give insight on emotional intelligence of the joining team member and overall cultural fit within the team.
